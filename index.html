What is Scratch?

Scratch is a visual programming language and the world's largest coding community for children, providing a simple interface for creating coding projects such as stories, games, and simulations. Scratch was developed by the Lifelong Kindergarten Group at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 2003 and was publicly released in 2007.

Scratch was designed to support and promote critical thinking and problem-solving skills, as well as collaboration with other people using Scratch. Scratch uses block-style coding, making it easy for beginners to learn and understand coding concepts. The platform also has its own paint editor and sound builder, allowing users to create their own unique projects.

Who is Scratch for?

Scratch targets for an audience of ages ranging from 8 to 16. People who are new to programming can learn all the basics of programming with Scratch in a blink of an eye. Scratch is strongly recommended for kids who are really interested in programming and want to make a profession out of it.

Scratch is for teacher who want their student to learn code in the fastest time possible and students who learn Scratch and then get into professional coding have a head start than people who did not. So, it is recommended to teach kids this beautiful block-based programming language.
